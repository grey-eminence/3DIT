Introduction

People spend all their lives in a 3D world and they develop skills for manipulating 3D objects, navigating around the 3D world, and interpreting 3D spatial relationships. However, they often find it difficult to interact in interactive 3D environments. Quite often, this is the result of a not properly designed user interface or due to inappropriate selection of interaction techniques.

In the paper published at Eurographics'2013, we have presented a comprehensive review of the state of the art of mouse and touch based interaction techniques for interactive 3D environments. The survey was created to aid both researchers and developers of interactive 3D applications in having a clearer overview of the topic and in particular can be useful for practitioners and researchers that are new to the field of interactive 3D graphics.

Testbed

Besides the theoretical framework, we also need practical tools to carry out the research and as 3D is becoming more popular on the Web, it is clear that we need firmer and more "hands-on" understanding of 3D interaction principles. Therefore, we developed a testbed that was designed for the experimentation and for the assessment of different 3D interaction techniques.

Taxonomy

There is a huge number of 3D interaction techniques and obviously no single interaction method can be considered perfect for any task and any 3D scene. Choosing a suitable interaction technique to a given application is not easy, since applications may have very different requirements. Therefore, in the taxonomy section we aim to provide a simple classification allowing a designer or a programmer to choose the best compromise for his/her needs.

By classifying, categorizing, and decomposing the techniques, we gain a much deeper understanding of them and provide ourselves with a framework that can be further used to help design new interaction techniques.
